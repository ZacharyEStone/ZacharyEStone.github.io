  1. Materials

    2. Vellum (HSOS), ii+134+ii, There are several foliations; the small, modern, Arabic numerals in pencil are cited, but incorrect as ff. 1-2, and 133-4 are likely later additions, if only slightly. 224 x 130, 142 x 84, 29-31 lines per page. Two scribes. Scribe A copied the main text in late Caroline miniscule hand of good quality and showing significant Norman influence. Scribe B inserted one major addition and made many corrections in a s xiiin protogothic bookhand very similar to several Dover/Christ Church Canterbury hands of the same period. Leaves were folded and pricked, then ruled on the hair side with a hard point. The first, second, antepenultimate, penultimate, and ultimate lines are ruled from edge to edge. Two vertical rules on each side. Writing normally ATL but copied BTL for on ff. 36r-40v, 41v-50r 59r-60r, 66v, and 105r. The first pricks are occasionally unruled and on some folios the scribe resorts to writing below the bottom line to keep roughly 30 lines per page. Punctuated by punctus, punctus elevatus, punctus interrogitivus, and middle dot.

    6. Additions

      7. Capitula for Mt. were slightly added after the main text by Scribe B. On fol. 65v Scribe B has added a note in rustics after the explicit stating that Mc. has 1800 verses. Langton's chapter numbers have been added by a medieval hand in pencil. Two glosses from the Glossa Ordinaria appear on f. 43v (Mt. 28:1) and f. 80v (Lk. 9:3) in a s. xii document hand. Excluding the last sentence of the gloss on f. 80v the two glosses largely agree with both early printed versions of the Glossa and many s xii manuscripts [Stone, 2010, Appendix E]. Illegible pencil/crayon s xiii glossing in the margins of 83v-84r and 85v-86r.

    7. Binding

      8. Modern, cardboard wrapped in light brown leather with gold trim and gold flowers in each corner. 5 bands, leaves trimmed, perhaps significantly in the rebinding process. Paper flyleaves of at least two different stocks, no watermarks.
